---
title: "If Not Now, Then When?"
date: 2026-04-07
---

Recently, I’ve been receiving messages from the universe—or Gus, a handy acronym for God, Universe, and Source, as I’ve heard some people call it.
For as long as I can remember, the Universe and I have been in conversation. Recently, though, we’ve been communicating less and less—like a friendship separated by distance that slowly devolves from long phone conversations into nothing more than “likes” on Instagram.
It wasn’t that the messages were subtle—it was that I was. Or maybe more accurately, I was disconnected. The messages were likely always there… until one day, I was ready to pick up. 
It was an early Friday morning. I was in a session with my usual Friday client when something unexpectedly grabbed both of our attention. I had set my phone down on the seat of a piece of equipment while I walked and talked her through the next block of her workout. About thirty seconds later, we both turned toward the sharp sound of my phone smacking against the rubber gym floor. The noise shattered the silence—and her nerves.
Before my body had a chance to react, she exclaimed, “What the hell was that?”
At that moment, I knew. The universe was sending me a clear message: Listen up.
Four days passed, and nothing else had grabbed my attention. But I’d been carrying on a one-way conversation with Gus—asking for more signs, confirming that they had my attention, and requesting that the messages be a little more obvious next time.
Then this morning, something peculiar happened.
One of my partner’s favorite takeout spots adorns every order with a handwritten positive affirmation on a sticky note. It’s cute and, with the right perspective, uplifting. Over time, my partner had collected about ten of these notes, and three weeks ago, I gave them a new home bordering our upstairs bathroom mirror.
Three weeks of showers, humidity, and cleaning went by. Not once did a single sticky note lose its grip or fall from the wall.
Until this morning.
I was in the shower, thinking about—who knows what—when a flicker of bright orange caught my eye. One of the sticky notes had released itself and gently fluttered to the bathroom floor, like a leaf in autumn.
The note read: “If not now, then when?”
Message received.
Instinctively, I tried to remember what I’d been thinking about just moments before. Surely this had something to do with that. I widened the lens and considered everything I’ve been putting off lately—and my mind was immediately bombarded.
I want to journal more regularly, but I keep “forgetting.”
I have sewing projects started but never finished.
Books abandoned one chapter in.
Hard conversations avoided with people I care about.
As these thoughts piled up, the water began to run cold.
I shut it off, stepped out of the shower, and moved on with my morning routine. Still, the phrase rattled around in my head:
If not now, then when?
If not now, then when?
Now, sitting at my corporate 9–5 desk job, one coffee and one tea into my morning, I’m taking the time to reflect and write—and I think I have an answer. It might not be the answer, but it’s a step. And I think that’s the point.
Tomorrow isn’t a guarantee. My next breath isn’t even a guarantee. Waiting for the timing to be “right” before moving forward isn’t a recipe for success—it’s fear disguised as a safety net. It’s my inner saboteur.
I want to live my life more fully. I want to stand on my own legs and belt it out from the rooftops, Frank Sinatra–style: I did it my way.
It feels no coincidence that this message is coming to me in spring—the season of birth, renewal, and new life. Every year, spring performs a quiet miracle. Plants that have spent months in winter slumber—buried under snow and ice, their roots disturbed by tiny paws hiding nuts and pinecones—rise again.
Delicate yet resilient, they open their leaves and petals to the sun and welcome a fresh energy.
This moment feels like a milestone for me, however small. A signal to move, to act, to begin.
